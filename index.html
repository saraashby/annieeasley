Annie Easley

computer scientist, mathematician, rocket scientist

“You can be anything you want to be, but you have to work at it."


Annie Easley at NASA / Getty Images

Timeline

  • April 23, 1933 - Born in Birmingham, Alabama
  • 1950 to 1952 - Enrolled at Xavier University in New Orleans, majoring in pharmacy
  • 1955 - Began working as a computer for National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (renamed the National Aeronautics and Space Administration in 1958)
  • Became a mathematician and computer engineer at the NACA Lewis Flight Propulsion Laboratory (predecessor of the NASA Glenn Research Center)
  • Became a computer programmer, working in languages like FORTRAN and SOAP
  • Developed and implemented code used in researching energy-conversion systems, analyzing alternative power technology, including the battery technology that was used for early hybrid vehicles and the Centaur upper-stage rocket
  • 1977 - Earned her BS in mathematics from Cleveland State University
  • The Centaur project helped lay the technological foundations for future space shuttle launches and launches of communication, military and weather satellites, as well as contributing to the 1997 Cassini probe flight to Saturn
  • Took on role as Equal Employment Opportunity counselor, helping supervisors address discrimination at NASA
  • Participated in NASA's outreach programs and school tutoring programs to inspire female and minority students to consider STEM careers
  • Founding member of the Ski Club, active in annual children’s Christmas play, Center athletics, and the Business & Professional Women’s association
  • 1989 - Retired from NASA after 34 years
  • Remained active in the Speakers Bureau and the Business & Professional Women’s association
  • June 25, 2011 - Died in Cleveland, Ohio at age 78
